# MATCHER.md — DIRECTION SELECTION
Use during: Matcher stage only

## PART 2: THE CONNECTION ENGINE

A connection type describes how two poles interact in a specific question context. Connection types are pole-agnostic — any pair can produce any type depending on the question.

How to use: identify the activated poles from the question. For each pole pair, ask which connection type best describes their relationship in this specific context. Then look up the matching direction in Reference.md Section 1.

---

### CAUSAL

Structure: Force A produces Force B as a consequence.

Logic: Pursuing A creates B — not by intention, but by mechanism. When a society prioritizes A, it structurally generates more of B as a byproduct.


---

### TRADE-OFF

Structure: Gaining more of A means accepting less of B — zero-sum within real constraints.

Logic: Resources, attention, or policy space allocated to A cannot simultaneously be allocated to B. The gain is real on both sides; so is the constraint.

---

### SYNERGY

Structure: A and B reinforce each other toward a shared outcome.

Logic: Investing in A creates conditions that strengthen B, which in turn makes A more achievable. The relationship is mutually generative, not competitive.


---

### INSTRUMENT

Structure: A is the means; B is the end.

Logic: B is the goal everyone agrees on. The disagreement is whether A is the correct or most efficient path to reach it.


---

### PREREQUISITE

Structure: B cannot exist without A coming first.

Logic: B is desirable and acknowledged, but it structurally depends on A being in place first. Without A, B is not simply difficult — it is impossible.

---

### SPILLOVER

Structure: Pursuing A unintentionally affects B.

Logic: A policy or behavior designed entirely around A produces consequences in the domain of B that were not intended and often not noticed until significant damage is done.

---

### BLOCKING

Structure: The presence of A makes B structurally impossible.

Logic: It is not that A makes B harder or more expensive. The logic of A, when followed consistently, eliminates the conditions B requires to exist at all.

---

### TRANSFORMATION

Structure: What A-advocates call progress, B-advocates experience as loss — same reality, opposite framing.

Logic: Both sides are describing the same phenomenon accurately. The disagreement is not about facts but about which values the facts should be measured against.

---

### FEEDBACK LOOP

Structure: A enables B, B strengthens A, the cycle repeats.

Logic: A small initial advantage in A creates conditions for B, which amplifies A further. Over time the gap compounds — either a virtuous cycle or a vicious one depending on starting conditions.

---

## PART 1: CHOOSING DIRECTIONS

### Step A — Gather Candidates

You have identified which specific forces the question activates and which connection engines it evokes. Basing on it, look up the candidate directions for your activated force pairs in **Reference.md Section 1**. Each direction has three fields:

- **Name** — the unique ID: `[pole_a]_[pole_b]_[connection_type]`
- **Argument** — one plain sentence stating the claim
- **Logic** — the intuitive mechanism behind the claim

Collect all directions that involve your activated poles. Do not filter yet — list everything.

---

### Step B — The Intuition Test

For each candidate direction, read its ARGUMENT and LOGIC fields. Apply these candidates to write a paragraph of the essay answering the question with the structure chosen then evaluate:

**"Does this direction feel like it naturally answers the question being asked?"**

#### The Freestyle Pivot Rule

If, after testing all candidate directions for a paragraph, **none** feels like it naturally answers the question — do not force a direction. Forcing an unnatural direction produces generic prose dressed up with a label, which is worse than honest reasoning without a direction.

Instead, **pivot to freestyle** for that paragraph:
- Write the paragraph using your own reasoned argument, grounded in the question's real-world logic.
- The paragraph must still meet all writing standards (tone, density, examples, word count).
- Every sentence in a freestyle paragraph receives `direction_tag: null`.
- The freestyle paragraph must still feel like it belongs to the same essay — coherent with surrounding directed paragraphs.

**When to pivot:** The test is simple — if you cannot feel the direction's LOGIC genuinely operating in your paragraph, and rewriting doesn't fix it, the direction is a bad fit. Let it go. Quality of reasoning always outweighs framework coverage.

**When NOT to pivot:** Do not use freestyle as a shortcut. If a direction passes the Intuition Test — even partially — commit to it and develop it fully. Freestyle is an escape hatch for genuinely poor fits, not a convenience.

---

### Step C — Select and Assign

Assemble 2–3 directions to form a plan for the essay where its paragraphs are most naturally aligned with directions chosen and the task of the question.

If one or more paragraphs have no good direction fit (per the Freestyle Pivot Rule above), mark them as **freestyle** in your plan. A valid plan may include a mix of directed and freestyle paragraphs — for example, 2 directed body paragraphs and 1 freestyle, or even 1 directed and 1 freestyle. The goal is the most natural, well-argued essay, not maximum direction coverage.

---

### STEP 1: IDENTIFY QUESTION TYPE → ASSIGN STRUCTURE

Read the question. Identify the question type and assign the matching structure:

| Question Type               | Structure                                                         |
| --------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Discuss both views          | Structure 1 (4-para) or Structure 2 (5-para with opinion)         |
| Agree / disagree            | Structure 3 (4-para) or Structure 4 (5-para with counterargument) |
| Advantages & disadvantages  | Structure 5 (4-para)                                              |
| Problems & solutions        | Structure 6 (4-para)                                              |
| Causes, effects & solutions | Structure 7 (5-para)                                              |

This is a fixed decision — do not deviate from the matching structure.

---

### STEP 2: THINK AND PLAN

→ Refer to **Thinking.md

**Selection guardrails:**
- For "discuss both views": select directions from genuinely opposite poles — A→B for Body 1, B→A for Body 2
- For "agree/disagree": primary direction for body paragraphs, reverse in counterargument only
- For "problem/solution": problem uses one direction, solution uses complementary direction
- 2 poles → 2 directions sufficient. 3+ poles → 2-3 directions
